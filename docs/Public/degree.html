Program Structure

Computer engineering program is structured across four years, each building upon the previous
to create a comprehensive understanding of both hardware and software aspects of computing.
The curriculum combines theoretical knowledge with practical applications through laboratory work and projects.
The program is structured to provide a strong foundational knowledge during the first two years,
with a broad focus on mathematics, physics, and introductory computer science concepts.
This ensures a solid base for understanding more specialized topics.
The final two years, however, are more sector-focused, diving deeply into fields like artificial intelligence,
machine learning, and network security. This progression allows students to gradually build expertise while
exploring advanced and practical aspects of their discipline.

Year 1

Calculus (1SEM 6-ECTF MAT 2314)

Develop skills to analyze and solve mathematical problems involving limits, derivatives, and integrals.

Physics (1SEM 6-ECTF ING-PHY 2414)

Understand fundamental physical principles and their applications in engineering.

Fundamentals of Computer Engineering (1SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 5614)

A comprehensive introduction providing global vision of Computer Engineering from both academic and professional perspectives, focusing on fundamental principles and societal impact.

Algebra (2SEM 6-ECTF MAT 2321)

Master algebraic techniques for solving equations and modeling problems.

Programmation (2SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 4324)

Acquire foundational programming skills using modern languages and paradigms.

Computer Architecture (2SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 3423)

Understand how computers are structured and how components work together.

Year 2

Algorithms (3SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 3411)

Learn to design, analyze, and optimize computational algorithms.

Database (3SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 2431)

Understand database systems, including modeling, design, and querying techniques.

Programmation II (3SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 5453)

Advance programming knowledge with a focus on object-oriented and advanced paradigms.

UML Fundamentals (3SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 6726)

Master the Unified Modeling Language for software design and system architecture.

Statistic (4SEM 6-ECTF MAT 2451)

Analyze data using statistical methods and apply probability theory to engineering problems.

Operative System (4SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 3425)

Study how operating systems manage hardware, processes, and resources.

Electronics (4SEM 6-ECTF ING-PHY 1111)

Learn the basics of electronic circuits and their applications in engineering systems.

Year 3

Networks (5-SEM 6-ECTF ING-CON 2121)

Understand the principles of computer networking, including protocols and infrastructure.

Artificial Intelligence (5-SEM 6-ECTF MAT 1212)

Explore the basics of AI, including machine learning, decision-making, and problem-solving.

Security (5-SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 2222)

Learn techniques to protect computer systems and networks from cyber threats.

Automation (6-SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 7654)

Study automated systems and their applications in industrial and technological contexts.

Concurrent Programmation (6-SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 5555)

Develop programs that run efficiently using parallel and concurrent processing.

History of IT (6-SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 4231)

Gain insight into the evolution of computer science and its societal impact.

Year 4

Economics (7-SEM 6-ECTF ECO 4321)

Understand basic economic principles and their relevance to engineering and technology.

Machine Learning (7-SEM 6-ECTF ING-INF 3333)

Learn advanced methods for building predictive and adaptive models.

Data Mining (7-SEM 6-ECTF MAT 3546)

Study techniques for extracting meaningful patterns from large datasets.

Final Project (8-SEM 18-ECTF)

Apply knowledge and skills to solve a complex real-world problem, demonstrating expertise in a chosen field.

Job oppurtunities

Computer engineering offers a wide range of career opportunities across various industries, including technology, finance, healthcare, and telecommunications.
Common roles include software engineer, hardware engineer, network architect, and data scientist.
Computer engineers are in high demand globally, with job prospects expected to grow as technology continues to advance.
Compared to other faculties, such as humanities or social sciences, computer engineering graduates often enjoy higher starting salaries
and more stable employment opportunities.
This is due to the specialized technical skills they acquire, which are highly valued in today's job market.
Additionally, career growth in fields like artificial intelligence, cybersecurity,
and machine learning provides computer engineers with a strong earning potential
and the ability to work in cutting-edge domains.
