Program Structure

Computer engineering program is structured across four years, each building upon the previous
to create a comprehensive understanding of both hardware and software aspects of computing.
The curriculum combines theoretical knowledge with practical applications through laboratory work and projects.
The program is structured to provide a strong foundational knowledge during the first two years,
with a broad focus on mathematics, physics, and introductory computer science concepts.
This ensures a solid base for understanding more specialized topics.
The final two years, however, are more sector-focused, diving deeply into fields like artificial intelligence,
machine learning, and network security. This progression allows students to gradually build expertise while
exploring advanced and practical aspects of their discipline.

Year 1

Calculus: Develop skills to analyze and solve mathematical problems involving limits, derivatives, and integrals.
Physics: Understand fundamental physical principles and their applications in engineering.
Fundamentals of Computer Engineering: Learn the basics of computer systems, including hardware and software interactions.
Algebra: Master algebraic techniques for solving equations and modeling problems.
Programmation: Acquire foundational programming skills using modern languages and paradigms.
Computer Architecture: Understand how computers are structured and how components work together.

Year 2

Algorithms: Learn to design, analyze, and optimize computational algorithms.
Database: Understand database systems, including modeling, design, and querying techniques.
Programmation II: Advance programming knowledge with a focus on object-oriented and advanced paradigms.
UML Fundamentals: Master the Unified Modeling Language for software design and system architecture.
Statistic: Analyze data using statistical methods and apply probability theory to engineering problems.
Operative System: Study how operating systems manage hardware, processes, and resources.
Electronics: Learn the basics of electronic circuits and their applications in engineering systems.

Year 3

Networks: Understand the principles of computer networking, including protocols and infrastructure.
Artificial Intelligence: Explore the basics of AI, including machine learning, decision-making, and problem-solving.
Security: Learn techniques to protect computer systems and networks from cyber threats.
Automation: Study automated systems and their applications in industrial and technological contexts.
Concurrent Programmation: Develop programs that run efficiently using parallel and concurrent processing.
History of IT: Gain insight into the evolution of computer science and its societal impact.

Year 4

Economics: Understand basic economic principles and their relevance to engineering and technology.
Machine Learning: Learn advanced methods for building predictive and adaptive models.
Data Mining: Study techniques for extracting meaningful patterns from large datasets.
Final Project: Apply knowledge and skills to solve a complex real-world problem, demonstrating expertise in a chosen field.

Job oppurtunities

Computer engineering offers a wide range of career opportunities across various industries, including technology, finance, healthcare, and telecommunications.
Common roles include software engineer, hardware engineer, network architect, and data scientist.
Computer engineers are in high demand globally, with job prospects expected to grow as technology continues to advance.
Compared to other faculties, such as humanities or social sciences, computer engineering graduates often enjoy higher starting salaries
and more stable employment opportunities.
This is due to the specialized technical skills they acquire, which are highly valued in today's job market.
Additionally, career growth in fields like artificial intelligence, cybersecurity,
and machine learning provides computer engineers with a strong earning potential
and the ability to work in cutting-edge domains.
